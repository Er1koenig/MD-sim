Part I — Paper Overview

This paper summarizes our MD study on tensile deformation of a CrCoNi MEA with a gradient nano‑grained (GNG) structure at cryogenic temperature. Full Paper PDF ↗

  • Large‑scale MD of GNG vs. homogeneous nano‑grained (NG) counterparts; uniaxial tension to ~15% true strain.
  • Strong strain hardening during the elasto‑plastic transition; after yielding, strain partitioning across grain‑size zones emerges.
  • Stacking‑fault activity (as a proxy for dislocation processes) exhibits grain‑size dependence and supports hetero-deformation induced (HDI) hardening.
  • GNG structure shows superior flow stress compared with the mixture rule estimate from homogeneous NG cases.

Part II — Grain Rotation Analysis Algorithm

A lightweight pipeline converts OVITO‑exported quaternions and LAMMPS dumps into per‑grain rotation metrics and a verifiable point‑cloud. The method focuses on robustness and quick visual validation.

What the Pipeline Does

  • Global defect mask: scan all frames; atoms that are ever non‑FCC are masked out for clean statistics.
  • Baseline orientation (0%): unify quaternion→angle convention (z‑axis) to avoid branch/symmetry jumps.
  • Per‑frame rotation: quaternion→z‑angle; fold into [-45°, 45°] to account for <001> 90° symmetry.
  • Main‑peak selection: 5° binning per grain; pick the adjacent dominant peak to trim twins/noise.
  • Relative rotation: difference vs. neighboring grains; aggregate by grain‑size zones (absolute values).
  • OVITO verification: export only main‑peak atoms (id, rot, x, y, z) for direct color mapping.
